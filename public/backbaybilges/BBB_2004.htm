The Schroeder Family (Added 6/16/04)
The Schroeder's have enjoyed some great times together this year beginning with a couple of trips to our little beach cottage on Bald Head Island - which we fondly refer to as "Iron City South." March took us to Ireland for 10 days to meet some of Berta's family and of course, we made time for some Irish dancing for Maggie! When we weren't driving around the countryside (or in the pubs), we chose to spend a lot of time on horseback....and to me, there is no finer way to enjoy the beauty of Ireland than from the top of a "harse"....as they say over there. Back injuries aside - we had a really great trip! It was back to Bald Head over Easter where we enjoyed stunning weather and alot of lying around, then it was off to ICFC over Memorial Day to be reunited with friends and family....one of our very favorite times to be in camp.

The "Irish-Jig Mobile" makes it's way North from the Carolina's on July 23. There will be a quick pit-stop in St. Catherine's for an Irish Dance competition on July 24. Maggie's feet are on fire these days! She has reached a higher competition level this year, so the bar is a bit higher now. However, if she keeps dancing the way she has been - she's sure to continue to do very well. It's such a joy to see her do well, but most of all, to just have fun! "Nancy/Mom/Nana," will join us in our travels to Iron City this summer, so we look forward to having a fun trip together. We'll all be in camp July 25 - August 13 and hope to connect with as many of you as possible!

The Sloane Family
Wally and Tallie again arrived in camp for a short weekend as soon as the ice retreated before the end of April. Funny thing this year - we were greeted on the dock by George Milligan who was supervising the start of the dredging of the new Centennial Bay channel. We took over his position, and continued with Chip Crawford to pretend to direct Moe in his endless churning, turning and burning in the excavator to get the complete job finished in record time. We can now entertain a yearly water-ski extravaganza in Centennial bay - boats and skiers never having to even exit outside the Reed Bridge.

OK, that was a bit of a stretch. It snowed and rained - a bit of it freezing - but we have to inform the members - if you have NEVER visited Iron City outside of the encampment dates, you might try to do so one day. The work that goes on when we aren't there is incredible. Oh, by the way - when you are there then, thank the Gregoire's - in all of their ever increasing numbers. We are blessed.

Tallie and I will be in camp with or without Sloan Boys as they become available all summer. In fact, we personally challenge any of you to be there when we are not, and a Crispy Crunch to everyone who has been there a week without seeing us. Kids included. Grandparents especially included.

For those who have not ventured to Monague and had a tour of our cottage - please make a point of it this year. We are very pleased with it, and are more than happy to share.

Oh yeah - family news. CJ is engaged to his dear Deann who some of you have met - date is set for June 18th, 2005 - at Wally World of course. Eric is "taking an extra lap" at UOE - having changed his major to art - he's blowing glass and loving it - what better a place. Brian has been accepted at Kenyon College in mid-Ohio, where he will try his hands and feet at college swimming after becoming a high school state champion swimmer in PA. Thank you, Pikes Peak!

Finally, based on our experience we ask - Could there ever on the face of this earth be a better place to raise your children than at Iron City - summer after summer? Come early, stay long - it is good for our souls!
