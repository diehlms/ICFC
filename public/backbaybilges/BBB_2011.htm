From Susie Williams on behalf of The Conservation Committee:

 

First of all, and with a little bit of sadness, I do not think we will be having a vegetable garden this summer.  While I ideally continue to believe a garden could be productive, we need to have a greater commitment (perhaps hire a gardener) to make it worth the effort.  I would be happy to re-visit this idea with other gardeners who share my enthusiasm.

 

The good news is that I will personally be sponsoring two new ICFC recycling efforts - athletic shoes and tennis balls.  Both are extensions of similar efforts in Pittsburgh.  Please donate all brands of athletic shoes.  They must be dry and have no cleats.  The more worn out, the better!    After being brought to Pittsburgh, they will be shipped to Nike.  Nike will grind and use them to make running tracks and playground mats.  It takes 25,000 pair of shoes to make a full sized high school track.  The tennis balls will also be brought back to Pittsburgh, and sent to a company that recharges them for use by tennis pros.

Donating shoes or tennis balls keeps them out of local landfills, and extends their usefulness.  Boxes for both of these will be at ICFC this summer.

 

The committee will also sponsor our second annual water level recording ceremony held over the weekend of the Fourth of July.  Our official measuring board hangs near the main dock, and will be dipped and the water depth recorded for posterity.  This effort has been spearheaded by Jack Huber. 

 

No doubt the committee will have other activities and is open to all suggestions.    So stay tuned.  Please address questions or comments to Susie Williams.

Do not despair!  There is already a waiting list for a second edition.  Twenty five commitments are needed to print a second edition.  Because of rising prices, the book will probably cost about $30, an increase from last year.  Please help the members who do not have a copy yet.  Order a second or third copy today.

 

The cookbooks will be sold in the ICFC office and can be charged to your account.  Please order the additional cookbooks from Mary Ellen Alexander at Imealexander@comcast.net  Remember, the books must be ordered, printed, and shipped to Iron City within a few months.  Please contact Mary Ellen immediately.

 

If there is enough interest, a 2nd cookbook will be published late this year or early 2012.  The second ICFC Cookbook will be published when the goal of 135 recipes is reached.  New recipes are needed, lots of them.  If you have a recipe or two now you wish to share, please send it to Mary Ellen at Imealexander@comcast.com at any time.

Border Crossing / Passports:

 

Be sure your passports are in order and up to date for all family members. Passports are required for all border crossings from the US to Canada – ground or air.
