From Bruce Lawson:

Boat Operator License / Card:

 

On page 15 of the 2010 Yearbook, it states “The Canadian recreational boating safety regulations now require proof of boat operator competency for all operators of motor boats. U.S. state-issued operator cards are considered as proof of competency. The Canadian Coast Guard Pleasure Craft Operator card may be acquired online at www.theoperatorcard.ca. The regulations include age and horsepower restrictions: if not accompanied and directly supervised by a person of 16 or older, the operator under 12 years old is restricted to a motor of maximum power of 10 hp; the operator over 12 but under 16 years old is restricted to one of a maximum of 40 hp. The regulations also include minimum safety equipment standards for all boats; these are strictly enforced by the Coast Guard who regularly patrol Georgian Bay. Full details are available in the office.” 

 

There is some confusion about who needs an Ontario Boat Operators License. It depends on whether your boat is registered in the US or Canada as well as the length of time (consecutive days) you spend in Canada. Most ICFC members completed the examination and now have valid operator’s licenses. Families are encouraged to have all children complete the examination as the process teaches safe boat operating practices.  

 

http://www.tc.gc.ca/eng/marinesafety/debs-obs-paperwork-paperwork_operatorfaq-2233.htm


Do the Competency of Operators of Pleasure Craft Regulations apply to non-residents?

The Regulations apply to non-residents if:

  • They operate their pleasure craft in Canadian waters for more than 44 consecutive days or,
  • They operate a pleasure craft that is licensed or registered in Canada (including rented or chartered boats).

The Regulations do not apply to non-residents who operate their pleasure craft (licensed in a country other than Canada) in Canadian waters for less than 45 consecutive days. Please note that a proof of residence will be required on board at all times.

If you require more information on how Canadian requirements apply to non-residents visit the Requirements for Foreign Recreational Boaters In Canadian Waters.

What is acceptable proof of competency for non-residents?

For non-residents, proof of competency can take one of three forms:

1.      A Pleasure Craft Operator Card;

2.      A completed boat rental safety checklist (for power-driven rental boats); or,

3.      An operator card or equivalent which meets the requirements of their state or country.  


To obtain the license:

Alternative #1:  Go to the following website: http://www.ontarioboatingleague.ca/page10.html   It is a safety course placed on the web by the Ontario Boating League of Penetanguishene, ON.  Before you arrive in Canada, arrange with someone on the list below to take the test.  It will cost $65.  The closest facility to ICFC appears to be Bill Demkiw at the Mactier Public Library  (705) 375-5219 bassboss@sympatico.ca

Alternative #2:  Take the course and take the test online.  Go to the following website: http://www.safeboater.com/online-test.asp 

 

This process can be difficult.  First, you must find a person 19 or older to be a Supervisor.  This person CANNOT be a relative.  That person must register through the above website with the Government.  Once approved, they are sent the test materials. You arrange a time, date, and place other than your home to take the exam.  The Supervisor submits the results and 24 hours later, you can print out your temporary license.  A permanent one will come in two weeks by mail. You must answer 70% of the questions correctly.

 

Alternative #3:  Residents of Alabama, Florida, Oregon, Pennsylvania, and Washington can obtain a license from their home states that will be accepted in Ontario.  Check your State website for details
