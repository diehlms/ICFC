August 18, 2009:

Dear Fellow Campers,

An additional advantage to staying a few extra days in Camp could be the opportunity to break records. Don Lawson broke a record today, a record held by Jenny Wilson since 1937. Don caught a 6 pound 2 oz small mouth bass on a worm near Pike's Peak. This is an historic day for Iron City and Don, as well as his netman, one Jeffrey Hugessen. He has much to be proud of - Congratulations, Don!!! Cheers,

Nick

Dear ICFC Members,

Our head camper said that "his enthusiasm exceeded his accuracy." Don Lawson's small mouth bass did indeed break a club record, but it weighed 5 lbs 2 ozs. His email is lmdglawson@aol.com if you want to congratulate him or get further details. It appears that Iron City really IS a fishing club. So, sharpen up your hooks and start saving your money because you will want to spend all summer fishing in Iron City next year. Hurray for Camper Days.

August 11, 2009:

Again I must apologize for the lack of Bilge updates. This week has been quite hectic. Peak is finally here. The numbers topped out at around 160. We had a beach day, the Tolley Cup, the Scott shuffleboard tournament, and the Sillers putting tournament is still going on. The Todd Cup and annual meeting are today, another hootenanny on Friday, and the live auction is on Saturday, meanwhile everyone is trying to get geared up for paddle dinner next week.

We had a great turnout for the Tolley Cup with 12 teams all together. The competition was tight like a new pair of underwear. Unfortunately, I was knocked out in the first round by Chas Stewart and Katie Scott, but I didn’t go without a fight. I’m still convinced they cheated. Mac Reed and Lisie Sillers took on Bennett Reed and Barbara Boucek in the final match. The crowd was going wild as they played first to win ten games. After some great games and several impressive rallies, Bennett and Barbara pulled ahead and won the tournament 10 games to 3. In his interview after the match, Mac told the press that this defeat was something of a life lesson for him. He went on to say that he would take a week or two off to reflect and then begin a much stricter training routine upon returning home in preparation for next year’s Tolley Cup.

I personally did not get to go out and see the shuffleboard tournament, though I heard it was quite the nail-biter. There were about 15 teams in all, and the final match was won by Barbara Boucek (the new Iron City tournament force to be reckoned with) and Nick Steffey. I overheard Katie Scott saying she had not seen pucks slid that well since the Summer Olympic Games of ’76. Next year I will be sure not to miss it.

We lost two of our staff members this past week. Stephen Firestone and Jackson Reed both had to return home for sports and school. We are expecting to lose four more this weekend, which is beginning to make me realize that the summer is starting to wind down.

That’s all I have for now. The weather is trying to be good for us. The sun is out, and it’s fairly warm. I’ll get back to you with some stories after the festivities of this weekend.

The Fab Five
Colin Andrews, Alex Johnson, John Bennett, Brita Bergland, and Katie Scott


July 26, 2009:

Whoever prayed for Iron City to finally get some warm weather, send me an email and I’ll buy you a Crispy Crunch when you get up here. The past couple of days have been, for the most part, hot and sunny and full of activity. And with camper days just over 100 and still on the rise, it looks like it just might be a nice peak after all.

This week the Collins hosted their annual Collins Cup (small-mouth bass fishing competition). Many people participated, but alas there can be only one winner. Steven Heath came in second place with his small-mouth that weighed in just under three pounds. And Marten Jenkins won the cup, reeling in a bass that was nearly four pounds.

We decided to organize a ping-pong tournament in the lodge on Thursday afternoon. We had a great turnout for both the kid’s tournament (ages 13 and under), and the adults (14 and up). For the kids it came down to a best of three match between Grace Firestone and Sanders Bennett. In the tie-breaking game, Sanders managed to out ping Grace’s pongs and take the gold. She claims that it was one of the best accomplishments she has ever done and one of the greatest matches of her life. The competition was very tight for the adult crowd, but again, there can be only one winner. It all came down to Jackson Reed and Todd Chapple, a modern day story of David and Goliath. Good vs. evil. Right vs. Wrong. The crowd was going wild as each hit was faster and harder than the previous. Todd won two games by the skin of his teeth (which is actually quite gross when you think about it. Skin having teeth, that is…but I digress). Jackson, no stranger to defeat, was a good sport and will have an opportunity to win back his family’s honor this Sunday at the softball game.

We gained three new staff members this week. Charlie Burke returned for his second summer with his younger brother, George, and their friend, Tom Coyne. We are finally operating at full strength, ready to take on the numbers at peak. Our last staff member, Elsie Widing, is expected to arrive later this weekend, which will bump us up to 17 wait staff.

That’s about all I have for now. This week looks like it will be pretty exciting, so I’ll have to keep you posted with the details. Adios.

The Fab Five
Colin Andrews, John Bennett, Alex Johnson, Katie Scott, and Brita Bergland
 

July 17, 2009:

Greetings from Iron City. This past week has been, what we refer to as, the calm before the storm. The high number of campers from the holidays dropped down to around 30 for a couple days. Camp was quiet…almost too quiet. Now it’s back on the rise and fast. Just shy of 100 campers, let’s hope this week they’ll be able to field a decent softball team for once. 

The recent buzz around camp mostly consists of the frequent bear sightings. It seems like almost everyone has seen one or more bears snooping around. Cathy Collins saw a bear with its cubs climbing trees on the back boardwalk.  The Heaths saw some on the coast of reed bay while fishing.  And John Bennett claims to have seen two adult bears sword fighting behind the lodge in what he described to be some type of wildlife duel (bears will do almost anything to protect their family’s honor). I, for one, believe him, and the idea of bears perusing about camp equipped with swords scares me to death. I have seen several campers walking around with whistles. In any case, it is nothing to get too worried about, but it should be made known that they are around, and you should be a little more cautious walking around at night when you get to camp. 

Our July hootenanny, led by Alex Scott, was a success and enjoyed by all. Guest performances included: Sasha Greendyk and Callie Deslaurier singing and strumming, Irish dancing by Maggie Schroeder, and singing by Riley Branch. Stephen Firestone had an original rap about his mink that he performed with Alex Johnson beat-boxing by his side. Mark, Dan, and Moe Gregoire from the maintenance staff all whipped out impressive acts, most notably was Dan playing slide guitar with a beer bottle. 

Another issue that I feel needs to be addressed is the surprising number of large fish brought in to camp and how many of them are not mine.  Cathy Collins reeled in a four-pound walleye…yum. Rob Heath landed a six or seven pound pike (biggest of the season). And Joe Bensy, guest of Chris Modic, has been more than successful out on the Bay, stealing small mouth bass from all the rest of us. 

The staff is having a great time. We took our staff trip to Parry Sound on Wednesday and made a trip to Spider Bay on the way back. Stephanie Nuel, Andrea Hylant, Jackson Reed, Stephen Firestone, John Bennett, and myself all took the leap of faith and lived to tell the tale. They like to wakeboard, take trips to Sans Souci, test the boundaries of hammocks by stacking person after person onto it until it tears in half, and dye each other’s hair! We had another staff member run out of gas while wakeboarding, luckily she was able tilt the gas can from side to side, collecting enough gas to make it back to camp…barely. Sometimes I wonder about them. 

Before I leave, I do have one more image I would like to share with you all. One that I am slightly embarrassed about, but it needs reported nevertheless. On Saturday night we had ferocious winds on the Bay. And I don’t mean just dangerous for sailing winds. I’m talking about tree bending, flag tearing, sweeping Jackson Reed off the ground winds. It had been brought to my attention that night that it would be a good idea to take the flags down to protect them from the wind. So there I am, lowering the flags carefully and holding on for dear life. I get the flags to the bottom of the rope when one whips me in the face and I lose my grip. And like that they’re gone. The ropes, no longer tied down, flew into the air on the end of the rope, dangling off the top of the flagpole. I look up astonished at what I had done. How would I ever be able to get them down?! I ran back to my porch to find John Bennett. We frantically McGeivered a very complicated emergency flag-rescuing device (tetherball tied to a long rope, tied to an even longer cable of some sort). We tried to heave the ball over the flag ropes…didn’t work. We tried to punt the ball over the flag ropes…didn’t work, and actually ended up getting our device tangled in a tree in front of the dining hall. Just when we thought we’d have to call the Gregoires to come and save the day, John mustered every ounce of strength he had. He embraced the power from all the wheaties he has eaten over the years and focused his energy on this one task. Using his delicate balance of grace and power, John was able to capture the flags and save them from certain doom. 

Other than that, camp has been running very smoothly. It’s been mostly sunny and breezy, the flags are back up, the McCarthy’s are gearing up to host an awesome Saturday night party, and we have no zombie outbreaks to report thus far. As always, don’t forget your reservations. 

The Fab Five

Colin Andrews, John Bennett, Alex Johnson, Katie Scott, and Brita Bergland

July 7, 2009:

Hello, everyone. Sorry you haven’t heard from us in a while, but camp has been err…eventful this past week. We’ve had one staff member take a trip to Parry Sound hospital (don’t worry she’s back and everything is okay), a blown transformer shooting sparks followed by a power outage, Canada Day, the 4th of July, and our usual Saturday night party and Sunday activities. And with our July hootenanny tonight and bunko night tomorrow, it doesn’t look like we’re going to get a break any time soon.

Two more staff members, Stephen Firestone and Jackson Reed, have arrived, so now we’re up to 3 guys. Yay! The entire staff seems to get along well and their normal activities include: king of the dock, sleeping on the swimming dock, wakeboarding, and often spontaneously breaking out into dance. Seriously, if you walk past the dining hall right before or after a meal and don’t see someone gettin’ jiggy with it, I’ll eat my hat (I think that’s the first and last time I’ll use that expression).  On Friday the staff took a trip to Sans Souci for ice cream and a quick dip at Teddy’s. Three or four hours later we began to worry quite a bit and just when we’re about to send a search and rescue party, sure enough we get a call that one of the boats ran out of gas and they were towing each other back very, very slowly. We thought it provided them with a great opportunity to bond and really get to know each other. Or at least it was a learning experience; I bet they’ll check the tanks before they leave next time. 

Our numbers in camp spiked over the holidays as usual, though still short from last year. I guess it’s because of that whole economy thing. The Sipe’s hosted a camp-wide cocktail party and chartered the new Iron City to ferry people from main dock over to their cottage in an effort to avoid the muddy trail that leads up to their cabin. I suggest everyone check out the new Iron City when you get into camp by the way, if you haven’t done so already. I heard a rumor that it actually belonged to Batman, but he had to sell it when the stock market plummeted. Nevertheless, it makes Big Ben look like a child’s toy.  On the 4th the Johnson family hosted another camp-wide cocktail party, we enjoyed superfluous amounts of cake in the dining hall, and the camp piled on the Iron City once again to drive over to Lion’s Bay and light off some big fireworks. 

On Sunday the campers somehow managed to pull off a win in the softball game. I will admit, however, that it was one of the most fun games I have played in all four years I have spent on staff.  The competition was tight, everyone had fun, and there were some surprisingly amazing plays made. Some of these might include Alex Johnson’s sabercat-like reflex catch. Kyle Stricker sent a ball screaming toward first base and Johnson stretched out without hesitation to snag it out of the air before I had a chance to blink. John Bennett forced a crucial triple play in the fourth inning. Then in bottom of the last inning, staff down by one run and bases loaded, Stephen Firestone stepped up to bat. He ripped a shot down the third base line, but Kyle Stricker managed to jump and knock it down. He picked it up and tagged the base to force the third out before we could get a run in.  And Jackson Reed tried his best. 

That’s all I have, folks. It’s a little colder today than most would like, but lunch is cream of tomato soup and grilled cheese. Boo ya! Until next time… 

The Fab Five,

Colin Andrews, Alex Johnson, Katie Scott, Brita Bergland, and John Bennett

 

June 27, 2009:

So here we are again, beginning the 128th Encampment on the Georgian Bay and happy as can be. I will do my best to inform you all about the events that have taken place thus far… 

Myself (Colin Andrews) and the rest of the “fabulous five” that are in camp so far (John Bennett, Katie Scott, and Brita Bergland) arrived last Friday evening and Saturday morning just in time for a cold, rainy Iron City day. We started our leadership training, led by Jake Morrow and Tyler Steffey, on Saturday night and finished up by Monday morning. John and Brita are now starting to get in the swing of things in the dining hall as headwaiters, and Katie and myself are trying to get everything organized in the office and ready for the season as coordinators until our fifth leadership team member and Bookkeeper, Alex Johnson, arrives.  The weather has been absolutely beautiful and to say we are excited and ready for an amazing summer on the bay is an understatement. 

Our wait staff trickled in throughout the weekend and early this week. It includes first year staffers Callie Deslaurier, Sasha Greendyk, Amy Moore, Andrea Hylant, Clare Bogart, Riley Branch, and Anderson Lidz and returnees Stephanie Neul, Courtney Anderson, Jen Collins, and Annie Lawson. Now before you say anything, I know exactly what you’re thinking. One guy?! We know. But don’t worry because Stephen Firestone and Jackson Reed will be arriving in a couple days, and we have a couple more staffers arriving later in July. For the time being I think John and I will just have to give him as much testosterone as we can, making sure to do plenty of manly things like grunt and play with snakes while he contemplates whether being the only guy on a staff of 10 girls is a good thing or a bad thing.  On Tuesday they trained in the dining hall before we went on a picnic, promptly followed by a canoe trip to baldy, which was fun especially for some of our first time jumpers. Did I forget to mention that some of our staffers have never even been to Iron City let alone baldy? In the evening we had a campfire on the beach and some musical performances by Sasha and Callie, who were amazing to say the least. They all finished up training with Jan Fougere on Wednesday and are now ready for the real deal. 

Our head chef, John Saunders, and his team in the kitchen have been on their game as usual. We have had five successful meals in the dining hall and many more to come. As a side note the kitchen is offering much more catering this year, including the platters from last year and adding a themed special events menu to the mix. You will also be able to buy your basic grocery or supply needs for your cabin straight from the kitchen this year (i.e. milk, eggs, bread, etc.). 

On Sunday afternoon, John and I spotted a tiny snapping turtle outside the staff cabins. We decided instantly to adopt him as our staff pet, appropriately naming him Bowser for his spiky shell and pointy tail. We let Bowser hang out in staff land as we ran to a quick meeting with the rest of our fab 5 and the intent to make a proper home for him when we came back. How far could a little baby turtle go right? Wrong! We returned to find Bowser nowhere in sight and not even so much as a note saying goodbye. It was a rough day for John and I, though we eventually grew to accept it and felt he deserved his freedom. Then on Wednesday the unbelievable happened. We found another turtle, Bowser’s cousin, just outside the dining hall. His name became Michelangelo due to the large resemblance to the teenage mutant ninja turtle. You know, the orange one with the nun chucks. Michelangelo was everything you could ever ask for from a turtle. All the girls loved him and all the guys wanted to be him; he had it all. Michelangelo spent two lovely nights with us, but last night we decided it was time to part ways. We released him back into the wild to lead a happy life and fulfill his turtle destiny in the world. We will keep them both in our hearts and their short time spent with us will never be forgotten. And the last story I will share with you all is one of an age-old tradition here at Iron City, the ringing of the bell before meals. Yesterday Anderson was asked to ring second bell before lunch. John advised him to ring it loud so everyone could hear.  Sure enough, after seven loud gongs on the bell with our brand new sledgehammer, the sledge cracks and breaks in two just below where the mallet head attaches. Anderson looks up slightly confused and scared at what he had done. Then he decides to continue smacking the bell with what was left of the wooden handle the full twenty times before giving up.  That’s dedication. 

Right now it’s about 75, sunny, and the water is up somewhere between 12-16 inches. As always get your reservations in and have a splendid summer. 

-The Fab 5

Colin Andrews, Katie Scott, Brita Bergland, John Bennett, and Alex Johnson
