July 28, 2017

The camp woke up this morning to a cold northern wind. Overnight the mid-70 highs had disappeared as the gusts sent the temperature plummeting to the 50s. Campers made their way to breakfast bundled up under coats, sweatshirts, and quarter-zips. Despite the chill in the air, a fearless group of picnickers endeavored to go out regardless of the weather. They were rewarded for their determination. The clouds parted and sunshine broke through to warm the rocks. The blue skies over head, the islands at O’Donnell’s were stunning.

As happy as the day has been, there was a bit of sad news to temper the excitement. Today the staff lost its first member as Jake Brown departed for the summer. He will be missed and hopefully will be back up next year with his quick humor and one-liners

.

July 26, 2017

After a week of beautiful sunshine, the weather has finally turned on the Bay.  Today has been gray with a chance of rain, but tomorrow promises a return to blue skies.  Last week was one of the quieter ones at camp.  At one point there were only 19 campers at lunch due to picnics and the low numbers.  By Saturday it had picked up significantly and started the climb to peak.  The growing numbers were not enough, however, to change the campers’ softball fate.  Andy Bennett turned in the best pitching of the year but still let up 7 runs to the staff’s offensive onslaught.  The campers did attempt a comeback but some fielding issues by the Chapple boys helped the staff keep its lead and its undefeated season.

With the improved weather, picnic season has finally arrived.  Over the past week Iron Cityites have visited Teddy’s, O’Donnell’s, Timmy’s, Pershing’s Point, Pomeroy, and the Umbrella.  Many have included the staff in their plans giving our waiters some much-enjoyed time on the water.  The most generous have even tacked on the traditional post-picnic stop for ice cream at Sans Souci.

 

July 13, 2017

The weather has dramatically improved over the last week.  The consistent sunshine has helped dry out camp from the weeks of rain.  Last weekend saw a very creative Saturday Night Party thrown by Matt and Lindsay Weissberg.  The games they brought to camp were tremendously fun and hopefully will find a way to stick at ICFC.

Sunday softball provided an opportunity for the wait staff to beat up on the campers.  With a score of 17-3, the staff again outclassed its opponent in all aspects of the game and ruined Wally Jenkins’ career ERA.  At Vespers, Gary Johnson’s discussion on a connection between Biblical dreams and inspirational ones led some of the young staffers to ponder their future goals.  Lastly, the Collins Cup went off without a hitch yesterday.  Duey Savidge brought home the hardware after a hard day of fishing.

 

July 7, 2017

July 7th, 2017 is a date that will live in Iron City history.  It was the day David Fritz seized from Don Lawson the most coveted of ICFC crowns with a camp record 5 lbs 13 oz. smallmouth bass.  Few could think of a member more worthy of the achievement.  Only the night before, David had regaled the camp with his fishing knowledge and secrets learned over the years.  Despite the monster he brought in, Mr. Fritz is still convinced there are 7-pounders on the bay so there is still hope for Don Lawson to reclaim his title or for another challenger to throw their hat in the ring.

 

July 3, 2017

Happy Fourth of July from Canada!  The weather has finally broke and it’s blues skies for the immediate future.  It has been one of the wettest openings in camp history but hopefully that is in the rear-view mirror.  The fishing this year has also gotten off to a slow start, but a combination of Bobby Reigeluth, the Fritzs, and the Applegate Clan are moving the count upwards.  The devoted musky hunters, Jack and Brian Huber, even netted an impress 24 pound fish earlier this year.

Saturday was Canada Day and being the senior Canadian members in camp, the Lawsons hosted a cocktail party in celebration of their nation’s birthday.  The Saturday Night Party, put together by the Douglass family, was unsurprisingly also Canada themed and featured the great northern talents of Justin Bieber and Michael Bublé.  The rain again spoiled the Sunday morning putting tournament but cleared up to allow the wait staff to deliver a crushing 14 to 5 defeat to the campers.  Jack Huber’s pitching was not enough to halt the offensive juggernaut that is this year’s staff, and Carly Phillips made sure to rub salt in the wound with her takeout slide of Bobby Reigeluth as he attempted a double play at second.  Hopefully some new campers will arrive to provide some sort of challenge in the future, if not the staff’s dominance seems guaranteed.

 

June 26, 2017

It has been a wet first three days of camp, but weather can’t hold back Iron City Spirit.  DeAnn Sloan and Nikki Wright Lusk graciously hosted the first Saturday Night Party on the opening night of camp.  Activities included a grand march, an elimination dance, and the ICFC classic of pass the orange with the Staff.

Unfortunately with a heavy down pour on Sunday, both the putting tournament and the softball game fell victim to the rain.  Sam Sipe gave an informative Vespers’ sermon focusing on the history of Iron City’s relationship with Canada.  Any camper interested in how the club’s view of our host country has transformed through the year should be sure to talk to Mr. Sipe.

The showers have continued into Monday but that has not deterred swimmers from the newly put out Bagel/Water Trampoline.  The weather should break in the next couple of days, and to the enthusiasm of all those here Wednesday looks to be sunny!
