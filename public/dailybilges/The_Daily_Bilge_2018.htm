August 19, 2018 

The last meal was served in the Dining Hall for breakfast yesterday and the 138th encampment is safely over, but much excitement continues this fall.  Below you will find a TENTATIVE timeline for the Bridge renovation subject to change based on weather and construction conditions.  The work will be noisy and a possibly dusty operation and folks should be prepared for that!  Do keep in mind that the Bridge site will be out of service and off limits due to its dangerous construction conditions. We don't want anyone to get in the way of the contractors or cause harm to themselves.  If you do choose to go to camp, please enjoy the process from a safe distance. The water in camp will be on until October 1st. If there are any further updates, I will do my best to keep you informed.

August 10, 2018 

There is nothing like Iron City peak. Although it has died down over the past couple days, camp is still bustling with activity. Since my last Bilge we have had many tournaments, a handful of meetings, the Hootenanny, and just last night we celebrated the Paddle Award Dinner.

We have had 4 tournaments in the last two weeks, the first being the Tolley Tennis Tournament. This mixed doubles tennis championship was fought for by 18 teams, all of which played extremely well. In the finals, we watched John and Judy Sillers play against Bobby Sillers and Heather Firestone, and all players were in it to win it. Ultimately, Bobby and Heather rose up and were awarded the winning plate that night at dinner! Congratulations to them!

Friday, August 3rd was the busiest day of this encampment. All on this day, we had a Scott Barnum/Ed Bartram Sale (Thank you to Missy Hugessen), the Todd Cup, the Hootenanny, and we had over 200 people in camp! The night before, two previous winners of the Todd Cup were invited to speak in the dining hall about their technique, and it was clear both David Fritz and George Stronach IV were coming to win this year. The winning fish string was ultimately caught by David Fritz, weighing in at 9lb,14oz, and he is now one of two people who have ever won the Todd Cup 6 times (along with George Stronach the III ). The Hootenanny that night was led by Nick Steffey, Fred Crawford, and Alex Scott and we enjoyed several hours of singing camp songs, eating popcorn, watching the Salt Girls, and enjoying the company of our dear friends and family. It was a very successful evening.

While all of the peak madness was occurring, we were also in the middle of The Sillers Putting Tournament. This match play tournament was played over several days, and we had various esteemed golfers and putters sign up. In the end Jeffery Hugessen defended his title for the 5th time. The last sports related competition of the summer was The Mary S. Scott Shuffleboard Tournament. There were 19 teams signed up for the event, and we were thrilled to watch the final match after dinner on August 6th . Fred Crawford and Manny Parody proved their shuffleboard skills at the end, and took home the winning title!

Although it is not sport related, last night we celebrated the coveted Paddle Award. After weeks of giving many of our staffers the title of Waiter of the Week (Jake Brown and Oliver Chapple won the last two weeks), we were very excited to give this years award to just one of our incredible waiters. Last night both our Maintenance Staff and Kitchen Staff were praised for a fantastic summer on the Georgian Bay. The grounds continue to look phenomenal thanks to Moe, Lisa, Randy, and the rest of their crew. Our new food provider Chartwells were applauded for their first successful summer – we hope they return for many more years. The Leadership Team was Rah Rah Ray’d for their hard work this summer, working as a cohesive team to make their small team of staffers successful.

The Leadership Team then took the microphone and started their speeches for our 10 amazing staffers. The speeches involved inside jokes, a poem, tearful goodbyes, and lots of goofy roasts.

After announcing all 10 of our staffers, the camp rose to sing Old Iron City and the following speech was read:

“This year the Paddle Award will be given to a staffer who we can always depend on. This person is very reliable, consistent, and we are so excited to see what this staffer does in the future. They came in determined to make their summer fantastic, and we think that they succeeded greatly. When we handed out the Paddle ballots to our staff, this person received an incredible amount of love in the ‘comments section’. Our staff wrote things like “[They] taught us how to be successful as a staff” “[they] were always on top of it”, “good one”, “great work”, and “The only real choice”. Ladies and Gentleman, the Paddle Winner of 2018 is Bridget McCarthy”.

Congratulations to Bridget, we are so proud to announce her as our Paddle Winner. She was given a celebratory Rah Rah Ray, and immediately was carried off to the Bay.


That is it for this weeks Bilge! Please check in next week for the last one of the 138 th Encampment!

                                                                                                            Rah Rah Ray,
                                                                                                           Camp Coordinator, Hannah Chapple

Paddle Speeches 2018

 Introductions - Hannah

At the beginning of the summer, we were all worried how a staff of eight would fair doing a job meant for sixteen. But as the summer went on, these kids proved that they were worth two staffers each, both as workers and as friends. It is our pleasure to introduce the tiny staff of 2018.  

Aleksandra Brozeski - Sanders

Hey Hey Hey its Aleks with a K. Aleksandra is one of the middle children of the Brozeski dynasty and as a fellow middle childer, I get her. She is deceptively quiet, patient, and the chilliest. Aleks is one of our best swimmers as well underwater eaters. For those of you who are not familiar with underwater eating it involves eating food underwater. Its not necessarily safe and its not much of a skill but Aleks had fun doing it and we support that. Honestly, Aleks is one of the most easy going, kind, loveable people you will find. Though she was unable to accomplish doing lights in under 1.5hrs. or successfully

glitter bomb the boys she does do a mean awkward Turtle. Working with Aleks was a pleasure and I can’t wait to see her working next summer. 

Claire Cunningham - Luke

As I got to know Claire, the foremost word in my mind was not “aware.” She showed up to icfc shocked to discover she would be a waiter for the summer, I watched her brew a full pot of coffee onto the kitchen floor, as she’d forgotten to put anything under the coffeemaker, and she was gracefully tricked into thinking the word “pineapple” was Canadian slang for what everyone else referred to as “spontanuity.” As I got to know Claire, however, I soon found a genuine affection growing, based in part on her virtuosic ability to have incredibly silly conversations w a perfectly straight face, her consummate knowledge of dance moves and her total lack of hesitation to call my mother a shrimp at the slightest provocation. Your guess is as good as mine as to why she thinks that’s a good insult. Nevertheless it ended up being the quiet moments on the dock talking about our families, how she’s grown to love iron city, or her fierce love for her new friends, that I realized that Claire is aware of the important things, even if she doesn’t know what to call that yellow spiky fruit. 

Noah Mackey  - Dylan

I’d like to thank Oliver Chapple for allowing me to meet one of the top ten staffers of the season, Noah Mackey. Noah Mackey doesn’t look like a real person, more like a generic caricature of a surfer or lax bro. Noah could often be found wake foiling, fishing, or setting the dining hall. Few teenage boys listen to as much classic rock as Noah Mackey. With his taste in music ranging from The Grateful Dead to The Cars to Bread and his immense collection of hawaiian shirts he can most certainly fit right in with the dads at any barbeque. Sadly, Mackey can’t be with us today, but we look forward to seeing Noah next year on the bay 

Bridget McCarthy -  Hannah

Bridget McCarthy came into this summer and from the very beginning she took her newfound “elder status” with pride. Bridget was an invaluable member to our staff as her daily visits to the office kept Sanders and I sane. This summer I learned a few things about Bridget, she is an avid plate breaker, and a Childrens book Fanatic. We discovered this Childrens Book passion one night when we caught her reading Dr. Seuss to a bed full of staffers. Bridget is one of our most responsible staff members, except for when she leaves staff land to watch the sunrise, or takes what Luke says with anything less than a grain of salt. I am so happy to have Bridget as a life-long friend, and I can’t wait to see her on the bay for many summers to come.

Oliver can best be encapsulate as a kid who acts like Winnie the Pooh with braces. July 27, 2018

We have had a busy week on the bay! Picnics, camp events, and staff activities happen every day! The weather the last few weeks has gotten pretty hot, but fortunately we had some rain this past week. Everyday more and more people arrive to the docks, and less people depart – we are gearing up for peak!

Last Saturday ICFC hosted a concert for the Friends of the Massassagua, which included a folk concert as well as a successful silent auction! This concert was a fundraiser to the support “The Friends” as they put their efforts into conserving the beautiful land of the Massassagua Park (which we are a park of!)

The Saturday Night party was hosted by the Coyle and Jenkins families, and it was a costume party! We had flocks of flamingos, a cowgirl, a robot, and a scary witch! We bobbed for apples, passed them between our necks, and the staff was tested on their ICFC Trivia! It was a large party, the entire camp seemed to be there enjoying the festivities!

The winners on Sunday Golf were Patty and Nick Steffey and Jaime Hugessen, and unfortunately the Softball Game was rained out! The Sunday Vespers service was done by John, Lisie, and Diehl Sillers, with musical help from Dave Bennett and Hannah Chapple. The service was centered around spirituality, and how everyone has to find their faith and spirituality for themselves. This service was impactful, because it really did promote that spirituality should not be “one size fits all” mentality, and that we all have our personal beliefs and doubts.

As the numbers of arrivals ramp up, the staff has been working more and more! We had two of our amazing staff members depart this week, Noah Petrick and Natasha Alexander. Both of these workers are tremendous in and out of the dining hall, and we thank them for a terrific few weeks on the Bay. Tonight, we will be welcoming back Finlay Gane and he will be here working on Staff until the end of camp! We were also lucky this week to be able to participate in two staff activities (thank you to the Brown family and the Brozeski family!).

On Wednesday there was a very informative Fire and Safety Training by Moe and Randy for the members and staff, and there will be another one this coming Wednesday! Please come and join us at 9am on the Main Dock to be rained! Also coming up this week are the Tolley Tennis Tournament, the Sillers Putting Tournament, a Barnim/Bartram sale, the Todd Cup, and the highly anticipated Hootenany (August 3rd!). We can’t wait for this busy week on the Bay!
 
                                                                                                            Rah Rah Ray,
                                                                                                            Camp Coordinator, Hannah Chapple

 

July 18, 2018

The boardwalk has been bustling with activity over the past week! Everyday groups of people head out to picnic on the rocks, to shred on wakes, and to catch more fish.

The Collins Conservations Cup had a great turn out, and the winning fish was 3lbs, caught by Mark Modic Jr! Carole Brammer also has been an active fisherman recently, every day at about 11am she swings by the office to show us her new catch!

Our new tennis courts are done, and the players are heading up in packs. There have been wages set and games to be won! These gorgeous courts are dedicated to Nancy Runnells, and the dedication is on Friday, July 20th after lunch, and we encourage all members in camp to come. We have just received our new Pickleball Set, and the camp will be hosting a tournament in the next week to introduce the sport to our courts!

Michael Hugessen has joined our small staff (bringing us up to 9 until our first departure next week! Jake Brown is the newest Waiter of the Week and as of yesterday, he officially out of his boot (he has been hobbling around on crutches since camp started!)! Catch him in a cow print apron all week, waiting up a storm in the dining hall! The staff also was able to tie dye with the Branch Family on Wednesday, and they all had a marvelous time!

The Saturday Night party was a “hat theme” and was thrown by the Collins Family! We had a ball…they even got the 20+ group to pass around a lifesaver on toothpicks! On Sunday the weather was perfect for putting, and jumping in the water after a crazy softball game! The staff is now 1-3 (I am not going to even tell you the score), but we all had a grand time.

We ended our Sunday with a vespers by Chip Crawford, with a wonderful introduction by John Boak, and we talked about keeping your calm when things get heated, and reminding yourself and others of love!

We are halfway through the season and are so excited for the upcoming events (Tolley Cup, Music in Massasagua Concert, and the Festival of the Sound!),

Best, 
Hannah Chapple

 

July 10, 2018

This past week camp has been warm and active. The camp has been lively and more members and families come into our shore every day. On Wednesday of last week we celebrated the 4th of July with a great cookout by our new Kitchen, led by Chef Iggy. That night after some delicious flag cake, some of the members and staff went to see the celebratory fireworks in Lions Bay (curtesy of the Bogarts). 

David Fritz, fishing wiz that he is, hosted a seminar Thursday night about drop shooting small mouth bass, which was informative and very, very fun. Hopefully all those who joined him have learned some skills that they can use in the upcoming Collins Conservation Cup! On Friday, we had the much anticipated Art Sale. It was a windy day, but it was all of the art in the lodge that blew us away! Some camp wide favorites returned including Scott Barnim, and we also welcomed some new artists to our sale including one of our own Alexis Rodefer!

On Saturday, the leadership team hosted the Saturday Night Party! We did a beach theme, and after a grand march, we played “pass the beach ball”, ice bucket relay, and Oliver Chapple showed the camp how he makes his favorite PB&J’s (with the help of Noah Mackey’s arms). Sunday we had our weekly golf tournament, and softball! It was a gorgeous day for golf, the winners of the day were Berta Shroeder and David Mills for low net (lowest net of the season so far!), and Jaime Hugessen for low gross! The staff “won” softball… the score was 0-0 because only 3 campers showed to play (shout out to Bobby Reigeluth, Jerry Rampelberg, and Dr. Bill Sager!)

That night we were treated to a thought provoking Vespers service done by Gary Johnson. He showed us all that even though we age quickly, that it’s the journey that really matters – and that we are all exactly where we should be. It was a beautiful way to end the week as the days up here fly by so fast.

Our new waiter of the week is Bridget McCarthy, who is a second year waiter! Bridget is our lovely flower girl, but she also works very hard in the dining hall and does her best to make our camp run as smoothly as possible! Catch her in a cow print apron all week, congrats Bridget!!

 

July 2, 2018

To our camp,

Happy Canada Day!

It has been a wonderful week for ICFC! The air is hot, the water is cold, and the fish are biting!

We had a few large catches over the last couple days, the largest being a 5lb, 7 oz. small mouth bass caught by David Fritz! Every morning we start the day with amazing food from Chef Iggy (the bacon is delicious), and recently we have been ending the day with Taps played by Dana Savidge.

On Wednesday, Oliver Chapple and Noah Mackey hosted a Chapple Rainy Day Ping Pong Tournament! There were 28 participants, and for the final match we watched a father- daughter showdown between John and Laurel Sager. Neither Laurel nor John showed any mercy, and that night John Sager came out as the champion (and the recipient of a fancy coffee mug)! The next day Renee Miller very enthusiastically hosted a game of bunko in the dining hall! We had over 70 players total; members, waiters, and kitchen staff – and it was a spectacular evening that ended in laughter and many, many, many candy bars.

The Saturday Night Party was hosted by the Fritz Family, the theme was Superhero’s! There were Power Rangers, Superman’s, Frozones, and a Wonder Woman! The activities were the grand march, and plenty of games for all ages (mummify the staff in toilet paper is always a riot).

We had a great turn out for Sunday Golf, and Kerry Todd and Sally DiNiord swept the competition, going home with all of the entry quarters! Softball was a great game (for the campers) (9-4), but the staff is working better together, and hopefully our team will rise up in the coming weeks! Last night, the Sunday Vespers were hosted by Sue Anne Morrow, and it was a short and sweet service with the main message being about the importance of saying thank you, being kind to others.

The first Waiter of the Week was Claire Cunningham, one of our first year waiters! She is a family friend of the Hugessens and although she has never been to ICFC before this year, she is doing really well!

Look for the next Bilge, next week brings new adventures such as the 4th of July, Book club, a Fishing Seminar, and the Art Show!

Rah Rah Ray
Hannah Chapple

June 25, 2018

To our camp,

The first three days of the season were busy and beautiful! The Sager reunion is in full swing, and the dining hall is full of missed faces. The camp has been prepped for months by our hardworking Caretakers, and their staff.   If I am being honest the grounds are looking great. The water levels are up by about 10 inches, and it is noticeable as main dock is flat on the water.

All of our waiters are busy not only working, but also running around exploring and learning all about the traditions that our camp holds so dear. There are eight of them this year, and they are frequently seen in the dining hall, the arts and crafts room, the tennis courts, and in their eight hammock set up in Staff Land. We welcomed back Bridget McCarthy and Jake Brown as our second year waiters, and we are excited to have Alexandra Brozeski, Natasha Alexander, Oliver Chapple, Claire Cunningham, and our two Noahs’ (Petrick and Mackey) working this summer.

After the very robust ringing of the welcome bell, The Saturday Night Party was very graciously hosted by Todd and Kathy Chapple.  The theme for the night was “Your Best Iron City Garb”. The activities for the night were the Grand March, and elimination dance, and a game of “thaw and wear the frozen t -shirt” which brought out the competitive side of our Waiting Staff. 

Sunday was a gorgeous day to enjoy softball and putting. We had 20 teams putting in the morning and the final winners were Sanders Bennett, and Howie & Renee Miller. The softball game was very close, both teams playing extremely well, and it was tied up until the last inning when the Campers hit a grand slam home run and all of the loaded bases ran home. The final score was 9-5, and staff can’t wait for their shot at redemption next week. Dr. Bill Sager gave a lovely Vespers service, centering his discussion surrounding a Tapestry he bought in Pakistan in the 1990’s. He spoke about how words and symbols connect all of us, and how symbols are just one commonality between all humans.

The Leadership Team is looking forward to the rest of the summer on the Georgian Bay, and we can’t wait to see all of our membership over the next few months!
 
All of the best,

Hannah Chapple (Camp Coordinator), Sanders Bennett (Bookkeeper), Dylan Martin, Luke McCarthy, and Harper Bennett (Head Waiters)
