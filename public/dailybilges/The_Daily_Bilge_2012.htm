August 15, 2012:

The sun is shining again and camp is slowly winding down. Because of school, I unfortunately have to leave a couple days early before camp closes. When thinking about this summer, I wish that I could go back in time and experience it all over again. The Wait Staff kept the dining hall in ship shape and it was great fun working along with Andrew in the office. I learned that making announcements in the Dining Hall is not the scariest thing in the world, and that a good attitude goes a long way. It was amazing to me how much I learned from my fellow co-workers. The moment I step off main dock, I will be counting the days until I return to Iron City Fishing Club. Rah Rah Ray!

Your Coordinator,
Elsie

August 10, 2012:

It is a rainy day here in Iron City and the campers are resting and relaxing on their porches. This week has been a blur of activity and fun, so it is nice to have a couple days to unwind. On Tuesday, the week was kicked off by Gregg Scott’s birthday, in which the entire camp was invited to his cottage for a cocktail party. Reece Jenkins, Fred Crawford, and Bryan Phillips livened up the scene by previewing their musical talent on the Dining Hall porch. Music was in the air and everyone was excited for the Hootenanny! 

After dinner, dusk fell and the merriment continued. Campers crowded into the Back Boathouse as the Savidge twins and Hannah Chapple welcomed the campers with their singing of “Hallelujah.” A highlight of the evening was the Salt Girls (Cary Douglass, Emily Bergland, and Eleanor Bergland) singing Jimmy Buffett’s “Margaritaville.” Led by our guitarist Chas Stewart, the Staff also sang the song, “Ho Hey,” for the camp. It was such a hit that we were requested to perform again at the Paddle Dinner! Overall, the Hootenanny was a huge success. Thanks to all who participated and made the night so memorable. 

And now for the moment we’ve all been waiting for…Paddle Dinner! The Arts and Crafts staff did an incredible job making decorations for our theme, “Under the Bay.” Molly Steffey lent us an enormous cloth backdrop of a bay landscape, which was stretched across the porch of the Dining Hall. Sparkling paper fishes weaved through green seaweed on the wall. After a tasty meal of stuffed pork loin and parmesan roasted tomatoes, staff introductions were made by the HR committees and our very own Headwaiter, Chas Stewart. Chas had campers rolling on the floor in laughter with his descriptions and quirky nicknames for the Wait Staff. Finally, the 2012 Paddle Winner was announced: Dusty Martin! Congratulations to Dusty for being a great waiter and helping us have the best summer possible.  

The camper numbers are going to drop dramatically by Saturday. Although it is sad that the summer is coming to a close, it’s been a great one.  

Your Coordinator,
Elsie 

August 6, 2012:

The power is finally back on and the camp is running smoothly once again! For almost two days, the campers braved the darkness of the cabins with oil lamps in hand. But with the help of our maintenance staff and coordination with Perry Sound, the power was on in less than 48 hours.  Chef John was quite happy that the fridge was spared in this power blip. Don’t worry campers, we will still be serving Nanaimo bars.

In addition to the power turning back on, this past week has been pleasantly busy. Many campers were putting away on the green for The Sillers Putting Tournament. After a couple days of grueling matches, Andy Bennett came out on top as this year’s putting champion. We also had another very successful Adult Dinner with an incredible buffet spread in the Dining Hall. Chef treated us to a gourmet meal, which included mushroom stuffed chicken and succulent pork loin. After working the dining hall, I was lucky to get a taste myself.

Since 1985, a member of the Todd family has not won claimed the title of the Todd Cup winner. Unfortunately, that fact remains the same. Don Lawson out-fished the entire camp by catching a whopping 10 pounds of fish. He said that it was the best fishing day of his life! Although my uncle Kerry Todd was banking on his three pounder to pull through, we were all ecstatic about Don’s success.

Tonight is the night of the Hootenanny and everyone is prepping for the sing-a-long. The wait staff is popping popcorn and sweeping the floors. The summer has been a success so far, and we have high expectations for the camper days to come.

Your Coordinator,
Elsie Widing 

July 30, 2012:

Hello Campers! It is another glorious day here on the bay. The breeze is warm and welcoming as it rolls across the water. The players in the Tolley Tennis Tournament were especially thankful for the occasional gust of wind as they hit their volleys and serves. Andrew and I even took a break from paperwork and represented the Iron City office on the courts. Although neither of us made it to the next round, I think we made Mr. Beatty proud.
 
This Saturday, we celebrated the opening of the 2012 Olympics with a bang! The campers were divided into their country teams and dressed in their national colors. Festive banners and flags lined the Dining Hall while people poured in for dinner. To top off all this national pride, our First Cook, Kaila Saunders, made a fantastic Olympic cake with sparklers and all.
 
The fun continued with the Saturday Night Olympic Party hosted by Team Italy aka The Steffey Clan. A highlight was the Nick Steffey’s lion hunt. Because of the Olympics taking place in London, the campers were given the daunting task of finding Queen’s Royal Lion. The party came to a close with the awards ceremony. Our ICFC athletes did quite well! Many of our campers took home the gold.
 
The one and only, Chas Stewart, conducted Vespers this Sunday. His talk centered on self-awareness and transcendence, especially focusing on the Universe as a whole. The campers loved it! It was something different and creative, and I am proud of my cousin for stepping up to the plate.
 
We still have the Sillers Putting Tournament and the Adult Dinner coming up this week. Not to mention the Art Sale in the Lodge. Peak has finally arrived!
 
Your Coordinator,
Elsie Widing 

July 23, 2012:

Due to the small number of campers up here, this past week has been pleasantly relaxing. The weather continues to be perfect without a drop of rain in sight.  Although there has been talk of drought, Moe and the flower girls, Kathryn Savidge and Hannah Chapple, have been making sure that the sprinklers are up and running.

This Tuesday, David Mills led the book discussion concerning the novel The Navigator of New York by the Canadian writer, Wayne Johnstone, While Lynn Edgerton may have been the only member in camp to have read it, the discussion was still very interesting.  On Wednesday Barbara Boucek won the annual Tom Collins Conservation Cup with a whopping 3 lb. 3 oz. small mouth bass! The wait staff missed the fishing excitement because we went to Perry Sound that day. Instead of bringing back a bass from the bay, the staffers stocked up on candy and knick knacks.

We finished the week with a fun Sing-a-Long in the Lodge led by the famous guitarist, Chas Stewart. The Staff is looking forward to things picking up next week…we are going to have 126 campers on Saturday! With Peak approaching, the staff is ready to show off their fine tuned waiting skills in the Dining Hall.

Your Coordinator,
Elsie Widing 

July 13, 2012:

The day is winding down and chatter is traveling along the boardwalk. There is talk that the Modic’s Sunday Vespers was a great success! The campers were glad to see Claire, Jordan, and Stefan Modic contribute personal insight concerning the theme of introspection. When I talked to my Aunt Lynn Edgerton this evening, we agreed that one of Stefan’s points was quite memorable: “find something that you are proud of…enough so that you could tell a stranger.” Reflection is an important aspect of vespers, and I think the Modics hit that one home.
 
Although the number of campers is declining at ICfC, we have been busy! With campers trying to squeeze in last minute picnics, the staff is eager to jump on board. On Wednesday, we had the annual Adult Dinner complete with cocktails and a delicious buffet, courtesy of Chef John Saunders. The menu included double stuffed potatoes, herb encrusted chicken, creamy fettucini, and chocolate-drizzled crepes for dessert. I had to taste some to see for myself.
 
Because of the small number of campers this week, the Collins and Hugessen family hosted a Saturday Bingo Night in the Dining Hall! I was not on my Bingo game, but many campers went home with a candy bar in hand. Sunday was a monumental day for the staff…we finally won our first baseball game! Sad I know, but last year’s MVP, Dusty Martin, ominously states, “We will dominate the rest of the season.  Be scared.”
 
The Tom Collins Conservation Cup is approaching and campers are grabbing their fishing rods. Looking forward to seeing you soon!
 
Your Coordinator,
Elsie Widing

July 9, 2012:

It is hard to believe that it is already the third week of camp! Last week was a blur of fun as the campers battled it out in the Ping Pong Tournament, the Mixed Doubles Tennis Tournament, and the annual dice game, Bunko. Due to the infallible participation of the Patton and Sager clan, both tournaments went extremely well. Congratulations to Alex “Wildcard” Scott for winning the Ping Pong Tournament! He showed no mercy to the wait staff while he dominated both the ping and the pong with his sandpaper paddle. Stephen Firestone and Amy Patton showed Wimbledon potential on the tennis courts as they worked their way up the ladder. After a heated match, Stephen and Amy finally claimed first place followed by Chas “The Man” Stewart and Lilli Patton.

Bunko Night was a blast. Renee Miller enthusiasm was infectious as she led the epic event in the Dining Hall. We had a record number of campers show up: almost 90 people! One could say that the game is all luck, but I beg to differ. I seemed to have incredible skill at rolling dice the first round; however, I think fatigue kicked in by the third round as I was struggling to come up with a Bunko. Since the admission fee was one candy bar, many campers went home happy.

This Saturday, the wait staff hosted the Saturday Night “Pajama” Party. The campers dished out their nightgowns and slippers; not to mention Mike Winn sported a fantastic Canadian maple leaf frock. The games included musical pillows for the kids and the frozen t-shirt game for the teens. Maggie Schroeder, a nationally acclaimed Irish dancer, also performed a fast-paced jig for the campers. She was amazing with her fast feet and sparkly getup.

The weather cannot get any better up here on Georgian Bay and the campers are taking full advantage of it. The wait staff can be often spotted on the dock with the diligent lifeguard, Bridget Irish, on watch. After a busy yet eventful week with about a hundred people in camp, the wait staff is somewhat relieved to have a more relaxed schedule. We still have many more events planned, such as the Adult Dinner on Wednesday, so come and hang with us!

Your Coordinator,
Elsie

July 5, 2012:

It is the day after Independence Day and the staff is still reminiscing about the Bogart’s impressive firework show. After the staffers piled into the Iron City with lifejackets in hand, we started the journey to the Bogart’s island. When the staff finally arrived, the night was buzzing with mosquitoes and laughter. Bursts of fireworks reflected upon the water as we huddled together with root beer floats in hand.  It was a truly memorable experience here in Georgian Bay.

This past week has been incredibly busy for both the campers and staffers. Congratulations to Todd Chapple for winning the Sunday Putting Tournament. If you win the putting tournament, Todd explained, you are allowed bragging rights for at least a week. We started the week off with the Martin’s staff activity: “extreme” tubing. Jack Phillips and I held on for dear life as Dusty Martin and Chas Stewart jumped like monkeys from tube to tube. Luckily, I have the entire summer to work on my tubing skills. After the weather cleared for Tuesday afternoon, the Chapple and Firestone Family graciously invited the Staff on a trip to O’Donnells Point. Apart from a couple days here and there, the weather here has been absolutely beautiful. If you are one of the four million people with no power this week, why not come up to Canada!

Because the Johnson Reunion is still going strong here at camp, the staff has dubbed this week “mini peak.” Instead of the standard Arts and Crafts, there was a Sand Castle Building contest for the kids on the beach…well the kids including Ben Johnson. Competition was high as the young campers built their motes and walls of sand. The campers received awards such as Most Original, Most Creative, Most Perfected, and Best Teamwork. Not to mention a sour key and the coveted Crispy Crunch bar. Overall, the day was a great success.

Upcoming events this week include a Ping Pong Tournament, Renee Miller’s annual Bunko in the Dining Hall, and a Mixed Doubles Tournament on Friday. Campers are looking forward to the Adult Dinner next week as well. Pack up your fishing gear and come visit us at camp!

Your Coordinator,
Elsie Widing 

June 30, 2012:

It is another beautiful weekend on Georgian Bay. The ICFC campers have been enjoying the Canadian waters as fishing boats and Ted Branch’s paddle board rip across the bay. Despite the large number of campers in the beginning of camp, the wait staff is doing exceptionally well in the dining hall. I have heard praise from Chef John Saunders and Souse Chef, Rosevelt Matthias, that the staff is attentive and ready-to-serve (despite the occasional baseball cap in the dining hall). Headwaiters, Chas Stewart and Annie Lawson, are working hard to insure that morning jobs and meals are running seamlessly.

With both Canada Day and July 4th approaching, the camp is teeming with excitement! Everyone was very impressed with George Stronach’s first catch of the season: a 4 Ib 8 oz bass. Following yesterday’s dinner, the birthdays of three staff members, Diehl Sillers, Helen Savidge and Kathryn Savidge, were announced with two impressive birthday cakes and a “dip” in the bay. After everyone dried off, the first evening games of Capture the Flag commenced. Younger campers, such as Oliver Chapple and Michael Hugessen, searched for the flag with the help of Wait Staff. The evening was capped off with a vibrant sunset and parlor games at the Siller’s cabin.

With many more arrivals today, the numbers of campers will soar to about a hundred people! Luckily, the Phillips are planning a great Saturday Night Party, which is based upon the popular movie and book series, The Hunger Games. We are expecting Brian Phillips to possibly show up with purple hair…I guess we will find out tonight! The Coyles are conducting Sunday Vespers with the accompaniment of Kathryn Savidge and Hanna Chapple as vocals and Chas Stewart on the guitar. It will surely be a weekend to remember!

Your Coordinator,
Elsie Widing  

June 27, 2012:

Finally, camp has begun! The Wait Staff is waiting in anticipation for the first dinner of the summer. For the past couple days, both the “Funtastic” Four and the 2012 Wait Staff have been training non-stop for the big opening of ICFC. Jan Fougere, who is assisting dining hall training, has insured the forks and knives are in proper placement. Chef John has been cooking up a storm and is excited about new menu additions such as the fabulously decadent chocolate peanut butter cake. Make your reservations and be sure to bring your appetite!

We also are expecting a large amount of campers for the opening of camp and the 4th of July. The Johnson Family Party is having their bi-annual reunion this year, which will sky rocket the number of campers this week. The families arriving on main dock today will surely keep the waiters on their toes!

It is a cloudy today, but spirits are high. The Staff is ready to begin a successful, funtastic summer.

Rah, Rah, Ray!
Your coordinator,
Elsie Widing
