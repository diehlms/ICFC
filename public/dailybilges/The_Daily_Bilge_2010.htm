ICFC Survey Results


August 20, 2010:

As this is the final installment of the Bilge for this summer, we have much to discuss.  First, to the communications committee!

 

Chip Crawford has asked that I remind everyone to take a glance at the Iron City website.  On the site, one will find the Member Survey.  This takes an average of 20 minutes, and is more helpful than you know.  Please complete a survey if you have not already.  Now, to the main event!

 

Paddle Dinner was Thursday, August 12th.  The waiters and kitchen spent the prior week preparing for the occasion, and it showed.   Chef John Saunders orchestrated a wonderful three-course meal, with the desert of “Memories of a Georgian Bay Sunset” as the coup de grace.  Many thanks and hats off to Chef John and his fine kitchen staff for another summer of pleasing our palettes.  We are grateful to have you, John!

 

The evening progressed, with accolades and hurrahs bandied about to the Gregoires and their respective staffs.  Thank you so much for what you do, because I cannot bear to think about where we might be without your effort and dedication.  Congratulations to the entire Gregoire clan on 20 years of service in Iron City, and good luck on the next 20 years!

 

Thank you also to Mr. Beatty, whose business savvy leads this club to greater heights each year.  I would personally express my gratitude to Mr. Beatty for his patience and presence in the office.  He is a strong-willed individual with an indomitable work ethic, and we are all better off because of him.  Thank you, thank you, and thank you.

 

Before we get to the big reveal, I’m going to direct some of the spotlight on Emily Boak.  Our resident musician on staff, Emily played the evening hymns on the piano nearly every night, and also performed at many Vespers services.  On top of all that, Emily was the one you heard each evening at 10:00 PM, serenading camp with “Taps” on her flute.  She is an impressive young woman who deserves great praise for her devotion and enthusiasm this summer.  I thank you for your service to Iron City; we all know what a special place this is for you.

 

As we whittled our way through the program and filled our tummies with delicacies, the only thing left to do was give away a Paddle Award.  Our winner is a waiter who truly exemplified the spirit of Iron City Fishing Club in their willingness to listen, learn, and cooperate, inside and outside of the dining hall.  This individual has pep in her step, takes pride in her job, and has a passion for the traditions of our camp.  After a meticulous counting and recounting of votes, the 2010 Paddle Award was given to Mei Raley!  While there were other nominees with many votes, Mei was our winner this summer.  As tradition dictates, Mei was handed the Paddle, congratulated, and then promptly tossed into the bay.  The next time you see her, be sure to give Mei a big hug or a high-five, she deserves it. 

 

Camp has dwindled down to our last few dozen for the season.  The days are still and the nights are clear.  The stars at night shine together in a chorus of light, pulling campers into warm dreams of summer.   The windswept pines, the watchful guardians of the shorelines, beckon our boats to turn around and rejoin them.  But we must depart and reacquaint ourselves with work and school, so that we may return stronger in mind and spirit.  May we all find health and happiness in the real world.  Enjoy the winter chores, and remember that tradition is a mighty thing. 

 

The Best and the Jolliest,

 

Stewart Prescott Bennett 


August 9, 2010:

Hello from Georgian Bay!  I am happy to report that peak is poking!  I apologize for the tardiness of this Bilge, but it was an eventful week.

The cooler weather has tiptoed in, providing for some lovely sleeping weather.  This is a welcome change from all the still and humid days earlier this summer.  Well, let us recap this past week’s adventures in Iron City:

The Sunday afternoon softball game was a lopsided affair, to say the very least.  All the big bats were present, and I believe a record number of softballs were launched onto the new tennis courts.  The poor tennis players were constantly dodging a downpour of homeruns.  It was painful to watch the camper lead increasing with seemingly every at-bat, but the Staff soldiered on, keeping the aspiration to victory alive.  The final score was a dreary 16-1, or in simpler terms, “Lots and Lots” to “Ben Sager.”  That’s correct; Ben Sager hit yet another inside-the-park homerun, but it wasn’t enough to stop the insatiable camper offense.  If only we could have a team of robo-Ben Sagers, and maybe clone him a bunch as well.

To continue our review of the other competitions this past week, the Todd Cup had the fisher people hopping into their boats and bounding through the bay, in search of all the fishies.  While valiant efforts were forged, George Stronach III walked out of the dining hall that evening with a smile and his name on the Todd Cup.

As for the Sillers Putting Tournament, the field of 20 was whittled down to the final match between Mac Reed and Alex Scott.  With a gallery gathered by the putting green, Mac edged out Alex to claim victory.  The dark horse competitor in this tournament was Jack Phillips, as he cut down veteran putters the likes of John Sillers and Cynnie Rampelberg. 

The Tolley Tennis Tournament was full of some great matches and fun teams to watch.  The new tennis courts saw lots of use because of rain the previous evening, so thank you to all of those tennis fans who helped to get that off (and into) the ground.  The final match of this tournament was the favored duo of Loren Musgrove and Bobby Sillers playing against the upstart squad of Megan Collins and Mac Reed.  Bobby and Loren were just too much handle for Mac and Megan, and are this summer’s Tolley Tennis Tournament champions.

Congratulations to all the competitors and victors, it was a fun week for sports fans!

Camp is slowly inching towards August 21st, our last breakfast, but there is still time!  Come for a long weekend, or the odd Tuesday, it doesn’t matter!  We have some exciting things this weekend with Paddle Dinner on Thursday and the Old Friends Night on Friday, and you don’t want to miss those.  I have a credible source who told me that Stevie Gregoire is a lead candidate for the paddle, based solely on his dinosaur and kitten impressions.  That’s all for now,  

The Best and the Jolliest,

Stew “Musky-Slap” Bennett


July 29, 2010:

Good Day, faithful readers!  I bring you glad tidings from a blustery Georgian Bay.  Boy-oh-boy, were the winds a-whoopin’ and a-hollerin’ yesterday; a proper July day in Iron City, indeed.   Camp is certainly trickling in more every day, but it isn’t the deluge of campers we’ve been expecting.

The Bushkoffs hosted a Harry Potter-themed Saturday Night Party.  Plenty of wizards and various other nonsensical creatures made an appearance at this magical extravaganza.  The last game of the night was a doozy: Harry Potter Jeopardy.  Featuring categories like “Characters,” “Spells,” and “Miscellaneous,” young people were divided into competing teams and pitted against each other in a battle of brains.  By the slimmest of margins Team Gryffindor pulled out the victory.  Personally, I was amazed by the wealth of knowledge my peers possess about this Harry Potter fellow.  I know that he is a magic wizard with a silly birthmark, and that is the extent of my familiarity.  Now, if someone were to propose a BATMAN theme for a Saturday Night Party, a certain Office Coordinator would be very satisfied….

Sunday was nice and quiet, as tradition dictates.  Well, I did bend the rules a tickle in order to pick up our newest addition to wait staff, Ben Sager.  After spending 36 hours in transit from Ashland, Oregon, Ben pulled into MacTier a weary traveler.  We arrived at the Main Dock at 3:00 PM, just in time for the weekly softball match.  By 3:25 PM, Ben was jogging into home plate, having just hit an inside-the-park homerun.  TRUE STORY.  Even with this astounding feat, the resolution of this match was more than a bit lopsided in favor of those awful campers.  The final score was “Many” to “We Should Brush Up On Our Defensive Positioning.”

Sunday evening Vespers was led by the Firestone family this week.  We discussed the significance of a prayer, no matter the duration or length.  A prayer is a prayer, no matter how small!  There was a slight technologic twist with this service, as Jeff Firestone was reading from his Apple iPad.

Well, we certainly have lots of exciting things happening very soon: the Tolley Tennis Tournament, the Scott Shuffleboard Tournament, and our Semi-Annual Meeting.  Your attendance is requested at ALL of these events.  Also, a reminder:  Please inform us office-folk of your reservations.  Chef John likes to feed people, and he also likes to know how many people to feed.  Come for the food, stay for the NEW CHAIRS!!  That’s right folks, the new dining hall chairs have arrived!  That’s all for now, but stay tuned for more to follow, dear readers!  Enjoy your summer and join us soon!  COUGH (batman-themed Saturday night party) COUGH.   Just a bit of a cough there.

The Best and the Jolliest,

STEW 


July 23, 2010:

I know this is a fishing club, but boy this summer’s catches have been incredible!! Not a day goes by without the click of stringers and the groan of the fishing scale outside of the store.  Beyond the normal wave of expert fisherpeople the Collins family hosted the Tom Collins Conservation Cup on Wednesday, challenging the campers to find the largest smallmouth bass in the bay. Lynn Todd Edgerton caught the winner, a whopping 2lb 15oz smallmouth bass, just off of Birch Island. Cathy, Rip, and Ina Sable kept the camp on their toes and brought in some bass of their own (Rip’s was 2lb 8oz and Ina caught a 2lb 14oz).

The staff returned from their adventure in Parry Sound exhausted, napping as Moe guided us home on the Iron City, but quickly revived themselves at the thought of an outing to Henry’s. The girls headed out accompanied by the kitchen staff women who were liberated for the evening to enjoy fish, friends, and fried food. Stewart helped cover the shorthanded staff in the kitchen, practically the next Gordon Ramsay. Bruce tied back his hair and took on the role of Headwaiter for the evening.  The staff boys will have their excursion on Friday. Todd Chapple and Dave Bennett generously took the staff out to the front bay and dragged them behind some boats for an afternoon or two, tubing, skiing, etc.

Our Saturday Night Party was Bastille Day Themed—come as your favorite French person, place, or thing—and lead by the Jake Brown family. The Modic clan surpassed all other costumes with their representation of Beauty and the Beast. The Brown’s required that the staff use their brain for their game, ordering five items by temperature (low to high) and  countries by coastline length (largest to smallest). Many came close, none were perfect, but they DO know that your coffee is hot!

Wally and Laura Jenkins lead a peaceful vespers service, bringing a favorite hymn from home to share with the ICFC family. They discussed the support systems in place to help lift us up like Zacchaeus had the sycamore tree. The staff needed a calm evening after a difficult battle on the softball field. We lost to the campers in a close 2-1 game. It’s encouraging to have enough campers to field a team on their own for once but the numbers can always been added to. Come on up and help out because the staff will not take defeat quietly!


July 14, 2010:

Good day from the Bay!  It is the middle of July, and Camp is getting quiet.  Too quiet.  So quiet, in fact, that the staff is departing for a day trip to the booming metropolis of Parry Sound this morning.  But Oh!  What treasures and mysteries await us in this Northern hamlet?  Moccasins?  Fried food?  Knicks?  Knacks?  Certain DOOM?!?  But anyway, we’re looking forward to our trip.

The weather is warm, and the humidity is certainly getting in the way.  We had a spot of rain, but no breeze to relieve us from the heat.  The water has been so warm that at this point, it is almost easier to go jump in the Bay than the shower in the mornings.  Yesterday at Sherrill Dock, the ladder thermometer read 78 degrees.  Yowza!

The Saturday Night Party was a Staff initiative, and included a variation of the newly-wed game:  the Sibling Game.  The real winners of this game: the spectators.  We ended with an elimination dance set to the tune of the Pittsburgh Polka.  “We’re from the town with a great football team…..duh duh DUH DUH!  We cheer the PITTSBURGH STEELERS!”  But anyway, Go Steelers.

The First Sunday Campers Vs. Staff Softball Game of the Year finally commenced at 3 o’clock on Sunday, and what a stirring contest it was!  An 8-0 camper lead at the end of the first inning hinted at landslide victory, but how these numbers deceive!  After a few strong staff bats at the plate, that lead slimmed down to 8-6.  The final score of this barnstormer was 15-12, with the campers snatching victory from jaws of defeat.  Jack Huber recorded the complete game as the camper pitcher, so please note this in your programs.  A good showing by all, except on defense!

Sunday Vespers was a cooperative venture between the Branches and Jay Douglass.  Ted brought the hymns, and Jay brought the thunder.  We discussed written sets of set of laws through the years, and how the previous iterations have influenced the most recent, up to how we can condense “self-help” motivation.  Jay referenced the Magna Carta.  HISTORY.

Don Lawson has been bringing in the bass like a master fisherman.   Everyone else just seems to be catching bug bites.

Please send us some reservations, we want those camper days to go up.  We hope you all were able to witness the Spanish win their first World Cup victory and watch LeBron slink down to South Beach, the slimy so-and-so.  As you drift off to sleep this very eve, I will leave you all with a final thought to pervade your dreams: “Caaaamper daaaays, caaaaaaamper daaaysss.”

The Best and the Jolliest,

STEW


July 6, 2010:

Wawa-Weewa!  What a wondrous, wild, whirlwind weekend  t’were!  We got hit with a 3.5 Day Blow, which required our July 1st celebratory Canada Day cookout to be pushed inside.  Burgers and hotdogs were a-flying – literally!  As if on cue, a dozen Mounties on moose-back galloped in formation through the dining hall during our rendition of “Oh, Canada!”

The next day, Camp held an honorary cocktail party and dinner for the Gregoires, the guardian angels of Iron City.  For those unaware, this is Gary, Florence, and Moe Gregoire’s 20th year of service at Iron City Fishing Club.  After a rousing cocktail party in the Lodge, the Paddle was awarded to Gary and Florence at dinner to the tune of a hearty “Rah Rah Ray!”  We will be observing this milestone anniversary all year, so bring your gratitude and thanks to Camp, they deserve it.

On July 3rd, Scott Barnim arrived for an afternoon of pottery.  Joining Scott this time were Derek and Tracey Martin, who offered garden art-type wares.  As always, the artists left very happy!  Circle August 6th on your calendars, as this is Scott Barnim’s next date in Iron City.  Pottery:  GET SOME!
That night we had a Saturday Night Party/Hootenanny amalgamation spearheaded by Laura Burke and Alex Scott.  We played some old standards, with a few newer ones.  Thanks to Peter Amerman, Ben Johnson, Dick Boak, Bill Sager, Jack Huber, and Sam Applegate for providing the merry melodies.  Everybody brought their own flair.

The NEXT day was July 4th, or its equivalent in the film industry, “Independence Day.”  We enjoyed the traditional Sunday Dinner, and attempted to get a good old, American-as-apple-pie game of softball underway, but campers were not to be found.  So we played kickball instead.  Gary Johnson led Camp in Vespers with an American Revolution-themed sermon.  We delved into the convictions and ideals of our Founders, while relating these characteristics to Biblical figures.  After Vespers, we paid tribute to two of America’s fondest past-times: boating and explosions (separately, of course).  Camp was invited by Clare Bogart’s family to watch fireworks from their cottage in Lion’s Head Bay.  Fun and colorful explosions were had by all.

Camp is slowing down a bit with the departure en masse by the Miller/Burke/Johnson/Sager/Patton/Savidge Reunion.  We still have plenty of shirts, sweaters, vests, hats, and Mr. Bigs.  The fish are biting and the trampoline is bouncy, so call or email your reservations to the Office Folks.  Won’t you come spend some time with us?
 
The Best and the Jolliest,
Stewart P. Bennett, Office Dude on Duty

June 28, 2010:

Ahoy-hoy!  So we are five days into and just about every facet of camp is going swimmingly…except for the rain.  Not that a little sky water isn’t appreciated, but we are on Day 4 of rain.  Show me the SUNSHINE.  Anyway, at this point we’d like to introduce the 2010 Staff, so that you know the name of the waiter who is or is not pouring soup down the back of your shirt.  The two Head Waitresses this summer are Megan Collins and Alexis Rodifer, both highly-decorated veterans of the dining hall.  Bruce Anderson is the man who keeps the books this summer, so use his skills to pay your bills.  Hallie Bennett is the Camp Coordinator, and is on top of the “this’n’that” that makes up Iron City.  Stewart Bennett is the Office Coordinator, and is sometimes referred to as “el Condor mas Fuerte.”

Our returning waiters are:

Clare Bogart, Riley Branch, Andrea Hylant, George Burke, Anderson Lidz, Callie Deslaurier, Sasha Greendyke, Amy Moore, Stephanie Neul, and Jackson Reed.

Our new batch of waiters are:

 Max Alles, Noah Andrews, Parker Bennett, Emily Boak, Sarah Greener, Josh Martin, Mei Raley, Peter Robbins, and Rebecca Sillers.

The first Saturday Night Party in camp was a great success!  Jordan Mills and the Hugessons hosted a FIFA World Cup themed party which included some competitive dribbling events and a pop quiz matching countries with flags.  Do you know what the Thai or Uruguayan flags look like? Sunday started off sunny and warm, making for a pleasant walk to the dining hall for omelets and cinnamon rolls.  Shortly after John and Kathy Sager cleaned up on the putting green, the rain rolled in and it spritzed on and off for the rest of the afternoon.  Unfortunately the rain meant that our softball game was canceled, much to the dismay of the staff and campers alike.  Evening vespers was presented by your fantastic leadership team and the piano stylings of Scott Miller.  We drew on the philosophy of the legendary Coach John Wooden, focusing on teamwork, since we've been thoroughly trained in the building of teams between leadership, staff, kitchen, and campers preparing for this summer.  We are looking forward to Canada Day on July 1st and the 4th of July this coming week and of course to see more campers!  Those spectacular new tennis courts are just asking to be played on, so get to it.  We love getting those reservations, so send them in!

The Best and the Jolliest,

--Iron City Office Hooligans


June 25, 2010:

To ICFC-bound travellers:

Yesterday afternoon (the 24th), a beaver dam was taken out somewhere along Healey Lake Road, which completely washed out the road.  It was closed until last night, when they opened up a single lane of traffic.  We contacted Moon River Marina this morning, and the single lane is still in effect.  If you would like to call Moon River for a traffic/delay update, their number is 1-705-375-5406.  We hope this hurdle will be resolved soon.  Travel safely!

--The ICFC Office

June 3, 2010:

President's Memorial Day message to the members 

May 15, 2010:

Fellow campers,

 

Tallie and I lost the Spring race to camp this year – Chip Crawford beat us in to camp by several days – congratulations Chip!

 

Camp looks better than we have seen this early in many years – the rakers have made it all the way out to the middle of Dominie Island already, and all the fallen branches, twigs, and pine needles are carried away to make way for the new grasses and flowers. The great crew joining Gary and Moe’s Crew this year are Mark, Doug, Joe, and a couple of very nice new young men, Jordan and…oh, oh – I forget.

 

Florence, Lisa, Jean and Lynn are already beginning to make the cottage cleaning rounds, and the water is being turned on to each cottage one by one.

 

We walked the camp from one end to the other with Jim Beatty on Friday, and he is looking forward to another great summer.

 

Gardens are already sprouting from Huber’s to Branches, the Trillium is blooming along Healy Lake Road, and Spring is in the air!

 

Construction is booming at Widings and Stronachs, roofing projects are being completed, the roof structure over the water tanks is nearly done, and Maurice scrambles on scaffolding, racing to seal the bats from getting in over the fascia of the dining hall roof.  Let’s hope that after many decades, his expert craftsmanship will do the trick.

 

I just had one of the most enjoyable half hours of my life.

 

I walked Buster and Holly down to the dining hall to get chives for our baked potatoes, pausing at the front of each and every cottage, remembering friends, wanting to see everyone again soon.

 

Silly isn’t it? The wind is blowing, the sun is shining and not yet close to setting. The brightest and clearest evening after the coldest morning outside of Winterfest I have ever experienced.

 

As cold as it is, Tallie and I fell asleep on the reclining deck chairs in the Reed Weeds in the lee of Monague this afternoon. We had our jackets off. We’re sunburned. Dogs slept in the grass. This year is going to be another fantastic one on the bay, so you had best begin making your family plans for several long and enjoyable stays here in the most beautiful place on earth!

 

We are so Blessed to have this opportunity.

We are all so Blessed to have Iron City in our lives.

We must keep it so, and tell our kids they had best do the same.

 

Wally
