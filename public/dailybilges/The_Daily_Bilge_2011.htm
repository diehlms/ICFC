 The Daily Bilge 2010
 
August 15 2011:

Greetings and salutations from the Georgian Bay!  Though camp is certainly winding down, we had a couple big to-dos recently.

Thursday was the annual Paddle Dinner, with all deserving fanfare and reverence to boot.  The arts & crafts chicks were meticulously slaving away over the decorations for weeks, and all their hard work showed that night.  Fitting in with the theme of “Wild and Wonderful Wilderness,” we have Canada geese flying in formation on the ceiling of the dining hall, daddy-long-legs on the tables made from buttons and paperclips, and a few black bears hanging onto the sugar dispensers.  Thank you also to the Commissary Committee and some very helpful ladies for arranging the lovely centerpieces and greenery on the tables.  A big thank you to DeAnn Sloan for putting together the programs for the dinner and thank you to Katie Sloan for putting together the fabulous name cards at the head table.  To anyone I have neglected to thank for your part in making Paddle Dinner so special: thank you, thank you, thank you! 

Once again, Chef John Saunders and his team provided a dining experience worthy of Paddle Dinner.  First, appetizers of crudités and onion sandwiches were served to the hungry campers.  Next, the big guns came out in the form of roast pork tenderloin, stuffed with feta and spinach, served alongside roasted red potatoes and steamed asparagus.  Que delicioso!  Finally, dessert consisted of butter cream iced sponge cake with strawberry coulis and chocolate-dipped strawberries.  We were swimming in desserts, as Brian Sloan generously donated the groom’s cake from his wedding in June, which was in the shape of the Iron City burgee.  Although it was too pretty to eat, we consumed most of it.  Thanks to Chef John and the entire Sodexho staff for providing ICFC with good eats this summer.  Maybe let’s try some bacon next summer?

As the evening progressed, we announced the 2011 Paddle winner, but not before we introduced the rest of the wait staff.  It has been a wonderful summer working with these young adults, and Iron City couldn’t have asked for a more attentive and cohesive group of waiters.  Congratulations on a job well done, and may the school year bring new challenges and experiences.  I hope you have all gained a new appreciation for Iron City and the people who love it.  Now, the definition we used to choose this year’s Paddle winner:

“The Paddle is awarded to that member of staff who best exemplifies the spirit of Iron City Fishing Club, in and outside of the dining hall.  That spirit of Iron City is revealed in that staff member’s willingness to learn and listen, share and motivate, and respect and care about the people and environment that make up this community of friends.  Kindness, cooperation, patience, and passion are the Paddle winner’s distinctive qualities.  Excellence and hard work in the dining hall are essential, but success is expected in the many other facets that make up a staff member’s responsibilities.  This individual’s positive attitude provides for a productive, yet enjoyable working environment.”

With this in mind, we wracked our brains trying to find the right person.  The truth is that there were plenty of deserving candidates this summer, and the vote was tight right up until the end.  In the end only one nominee received the most votes, and I am pleased to share that the 2011 Paddle winner is none other than Josh “Bearpaw” Martin!  I am personally pleased (but not as pleased as Josh) with the results of the voting, so be sure to congratulate Josh and rest of the Martin family.  Along with announcing Josh, we introduced the rest of staff ahead of that, and I will include these blurbs for the website.

After such an exciting evening, the only thing that could have followed the next day was an Old Friends Night down at the back boathouse.  All the players were in place, and we strolled through the standards with ease.  A cast of thousands helped serenade the camp, with Bryan and Liza Phillips, Fred Crawford, Nick Steffey, David Morrow, and Jack Huber leading the way.  Thank you to all the featured performers, like Mark Groulx and Devon Sawin, who brought so much spirit to the evening.

Only a few more days left and camp is getting quiet, just as the weather broke from the heat of last week.  Fred Crawford has been entertaining us on the piano daily as we walk in for meals.  Quiet camp is a different animal, and a friendly animal at that.  I am sorry you can’t finish out the summer with the rest of us, but we’ll keep you in our thoughts.

Cheers,

Stewart P. Bennett


August 10 2011:

Peak has officially poked, and how!  We had 180 members in the dining hall the other night, and we’ll be staying on that plateau for the rest of the week.  There’s some sort of “Paddle Dinner” on Thursday, but I should probably figure out what all that entails, and where I can find a paddle. 

First, let me take you to the Sunday softball field; I have never been a part of a more riveting softball game, through and through.  The campers took an early lead with a couple heavy bats and Mac Reed on the mound.  By the end of the second inning, the staff were down 4-0 and feeling pretty glum.  Next time up at bat, we got our mojo back and packed the bases with Zach up to bat.  At this point I’d like to apologize to the tennis players on the far court, because this is when we started to ruin your game.  Zach sized up the first pitch he saw and belted a homer that one-hopped onto the back tennis court, 4 runs in, tie game.  Staff and campers went back and forth and we found ourselves behind 10-9.  Though nearly incredulous by the fact that we still had a chance at victory in an August softball game, we kept our cool at bat and focused on the final half-inning.  Ben Sager up first: base hit.  Zach Martin up next: base hit, two on, no outs.  Andrew Lawson, as if reenacting a scene from “The Natural,” nonchalantly walked to the plate and effortlessly swatted the ball to the back tennis courts, driving both men on base home and walking off to seal the victory!  Huzzah!  Great success!  Down goes Frazier!  Staff erupted and greeted Andrew at home plate with a joyous embrace.  Though they lost, the campers didn’t seem too put out, considering the thrilling turn of events.   I told you we had a good team, but you didn’t want to believe it.

The humidity was cranked up last week, but the bay feels as refreshing as it ever has.  It is pretty humorous to see everyone simultaneously feel the 3 o’clock heat and shuffle off to a dock or the beach.  The really sweaty ones jump in and out, but the smart ones know to stay in the water.

Congratulations to Bobby Sillers, the winner of the 2011 Sillers Putting Tournment (merely a coincidence) and to Bob Boucek, the champion of this summer’s Todd Cup.  Along with those skilled gents, Rick Bergland and Alex Scott came away victorious in the Mary S. Scott Memorial Shuffleboard Tournament (another coincidence).  It seems like every other day we’ve had an athletic contest of some sort, but folks are still finding plenty of time for fishing.  According to Chef John, he and Sous-chef Nigel Hughes have cleaned and served over 90 fish so far this summer.  Hardly anyone has shared their catches with the Camp Coordinator, but they certainly look delicious.  Keep bringing in the keepers, but let’s save some for next summer, too.

We still have over a week left if you’d like to make the short jaunt across the border to enjoy the bay, we’d be happy to have you.  Stay classy. 

Cheers,

Stewart P. Bennett, God of Thunder 

August 3 2011:

Greetings one and all!  I write to you today from an ever-filling camp with peak right around the proverbial corner.  We have a nearly-full dining hall, but plenty of room left for you stragglers who have yet to make the journey this summer.  It’s hard to believe that it’s already August, this is when we start running out of summer.

This past weekend was filled with lots of activity, capping with the staff vs. campers softball game.  Since I have not been able to brag much about our prowess on the softball field, I shall use this space to do just that.  The game began normally, but then all the pent-up frustration from losing earlier games unleashed itself in the form of a camper batting clinic.  The staff was slapping softballs this way and that, often touching down on the back tennis courts.  The final tally was 14-1, staff victorious.  To be fair, much of this had to do with poor camper turn-out, so it’s their own fault.  Of note, the one lone run was scored by Judy Sillers, rounding the bases in softball-approved flippy-floppys.  That’s right campers, we’re on a 1 game win streak, flying high and taking all comers.

Perhaps the campers decided to opt out of softball on Sunday in order to rest their arms for the Tolley Tennis Tournament on Monday.  A variety of players signed up, and spectators lined the courts waiting for the final matchup.  The grand finale came down to the Duncan Anderson and Anne Stronach team facing off against the Lauren Musgrove and John Sillers squad.  An evenly-matched game if I’ve ever seen one, and fun to watch.  At the end of the day, the Musgrove-Sillers squad emerged victorious.  Congrats to the champs and all those who fought off the bugs in this year’s Tolley Tennis Tournament!

The second Adult Dinner was held last night, with nearly everyone in camp enjoying the hors d’oeuvres and masterfully prepared meal.  Both of the dinners were great successes, so maybe we can do this again next summer.  Thank you to Chef John for the delicious food and thank you to the Commissary Committee for sprucing up the dining hall, a high-class dinner indeed!

Hope everyone is staying cool.  We’re currently sitting in a big downpour, and I couldn’t be happier: perfect weather for reading and relaxing. 

Cheers,

Stewart Prescott Bennett, Cruise Director
 

July 27 2011:

Dear readers: The heat and humidity has given way to a glorious breeze here on the bay.  In fact, this breeze would be better classified as furious gusts of wind.  It’s a shame we don’t have any sailors in camp, because these past few days have been absolutely perfect for sailing.  We took the staff boys and girls out to Henry’s on Monday and Tuesday night, respectively, and had difficulty taming the open.  But we’re tough folks, and we braved the waves in order to get to our destination of fried delights.  Thank you again to all those kind donors to the staff fund, we appreciate your generosity.

This week’s Saturday Night Party was hosted by Todd and Kathy Chapple with a pirate theme in mind.  After a staff game pie fiasco, we had to swab the deck (pirate reference) in order to continue with the party.  Sunday Vespers was a staff-run event, featuring the musical styling of Jim Kinnear on the piano.  After failing to come up an inspiring and profound message, I turned to Charlie Burke.  Earlier this year, Charlie delivered a sermon at a youth service at the Fox Chapel Presbyterian Church in Pittsburgh.  Upon reading a transcript of said sermon, and gaining permission, I shared Charlie’s sermon with those gathered at Vespers.  Even though my abilities as an orator pale in comparison to his, Charlie’s words and message shined through clearly.  Thank you to Charlie, and if you are interested in seeing the manuscript, head on over to this website: http://www.fcpc.us/newsite/CharlieBurkesMessage52211FallinginLove.htm

Lots and lots of events on the docket for the first week of August that you don’t want to miss: the Sillers Putting Tournament, the Tolley Tennis Tournament, the Semi-Annual Meeting, the second Adult Dinner, and another Art Day.  You would be silly to miss any of these, just downright silly.

Please come and visit, Chef John and his marvelous staff have some tasty meals up their sleeves.  For those of you who did not make the previous Adult Dinner, you are in for a treat on August 5th.  Be sure to sign up for that when you get in.  Call the office with reservations and stay cool, friends.

Cheers,

Stewart P. Bennett, CEO of Wayne Enterprises


July 21 2011:

Stop.  Just stop right there.  It is too hot and it is too humid to even think at the moment.   Our once-plentiful breeze from the bay has been replaced by the smoldering wall of heat.  Satan himself called me in the office today, and he wants his weather back.  Just yesterday the staff took our annual trip to Parry Sound, enjoyed a meal we didn’t have to serve, and baked in the sun.  This is no complaint, mind you, just a simple observation that some kids came back to camp in liquid form.  Once Moe returned us safe and sound, the staff made a beeline for the bay and stayed there.  If I had my druthers, I would have moved my bed out to the floating dock so that I could submerge myself in the blissful relief the bay provides.  Regardless, thank you to all who have donated so generously to the Staff FUNd (see what I did there).  It was a great trip and we appreciate those kind campers by being really great waiters and by not screwing up a great number of breakfast orders.

The Collins Cup was held yesterday, with tremendous efforts by all who participated.  The summer thus far has been great for fishing, and did not disappoint during a great fishing contest.  It seemed that Bob Boucek was ready to engrave his name onto the cup when at the last moment, Marten Jenkins Jr. brought in a beautiful bass that sealed the day.  Congratulations to the recipient of the 2011 Collins Cup and all who fished those glorious fish.

Following right on the heels of that major fishing tournament was today’s Crispy Crunch Fishing Contest.  A great turnout by the 14-and-under crowd at the back bay, and this crew caught almost as many fish as the big kids in the Collins Cup.  Conservation was the name of the game, and our participants followed this tenet closely.  Crawford Stricker, a born fisherman, reeled in a great bass that snagged him the Crispiest of all the Crunches.  However, once the time allotted for the contest had elapsed, a few dedicated youngsters stayed put and cast out another line in the hopes of continuing their success.  Michael Hugessen’s instincts paid off and in a big way, so congratulations to all these once and future fisherpeople.

You know, this edition of the Daily Bilge pretty much writes itself: Had enough with obscenely high temperatures?  Come to the bay.  Want to jump off of a dock?  Come to the bay?  Need more time to read?  Come to the bay.  Tired of going days without playing a single match of bocce?  Come to the bay, plain and simple, done and done.  Don’t forget to send me those reservations.  August numbers are low at the moment, but you can fix that, right?  Farewell for now, and stay cool until you join us.

Cheers,

Stewart H.W. Prescott Bennett, Certified Superhero and Crime Fighter Extraordinaire
 

July 18 2011:

Greetings, faithful readers!  I write to you now fresh from the Sunday softball game, shining and victorious.  The campers fielded a deep team this week, even bringing in a couple ringers, but the staff prevailed with solid fielding and tie-dyed shirts.  As a result, campers had to clear their own dishes this evening.  Be careful, dear campers, this might become a habit. 

Afterwards, the Collins and Bushkoff families led the camp in Vespers, dedicating the newly refinished lectern (beautifully done by Florence) in the memory of our late Anne Collins.  Though we miss her greatly, her presence will never be forgotten.  Keep the Collins family in your thoughts this summer.

On the subject of Cathy Collins, she has been bringing in some great fish, including an 8 pound pickerel just the other day.  Reece Jenkins is bringing in good fish as well, but they don’t really count until Wednesday the 20th when the Collins Cup is held.  Grab some worms in the store, them fishies is hungry.

Gregg Scott has been an excellent camp counselor this summer!  Last week, he arranged for Wendy Cooper from the Georgian Bay Land Trust to come and lead us on a nature walk back by Twosers.  Wendy dropped some knowledge about the bay area wetlands and some of the flora and fauna in our neck of the woods.  The next day, Gregg held a viewing in the lodge of a dvd on the history of the Georgian Bay region, ranging from the movement of the First Nation through the territory to the colonization of Parry Sound by some guy named “Beatty.”  It is quite a thorough history and I officially endorse this product as interesting and educational. 

The weather has been humid and just a little sticky, but makes the bay that much more refreshing.  Camp is starting to fill in, and camper days are the goal, so make your reservations sooner rather than later.  The Crawfords are doing their part for camper days with a family reunion.  Like my mother always told me, “camper days.”  On a related note, camper days.  Also: camper days.  See you soonsies, and congratulations to all of our Iron City marriages this summer (so far)!

Cheers,

Stewart “Baratheon” Bennett, Esq.


July 11 2011:

Greetings from the glorious Georgian Bay!  Scribing the first edition of the 2011 Daily Bilge is your Coordinator for the summer, Stewart Bennett.  I am honored to again be in this position and I hope that I can provide a glimpse into the goings-on around camp.  Right off the bat, I apologize for the tardiness of this post, I will not make it a habit.

Firstly, thank you to Mr. Beatty and the Gregoires for putting in the time and effort that makes Iron City beautiful every summer.  Secondly, a special congratulations to Gary and Florence Gregoire as they celebrated their 40th anniversary this past Tuesday.  Quite a milestone and we wish them many more years of happiness! 

Welcome to the 130th encampment of the Iron City Fishing Club!

After a little over a week since camp opened on the 29th, the weather on the bay has been splendidly sunny and only a tad chilly at night.  The afternoons have been a bit still, but all the more inviting for a dip.  It appears we have some rain coming our way this week, but we’re probably due.

We had busy past week, what with Canada Day, Independence Day, and the newest addition to your summer cruise, the Adult Dinner.  The Commissary Committee has planned, with Chef John Saunders’ expertise, two delightful evenings filled with hors d’oeuvres in the Old Friends Boathouse, sophisticated conversation, and fine food.  For only $10, you too can join us for our next Adult Dinner on August 2nd.  Our first of the two was held on July 5th and received high praise from those in attendance.

The first Saturday Night Party of the year was hosted by the Strickers and the Weissbergs with a “Red and White and Red, White and Blue” theme.  Lots of smaller kids went home with more candy that night, so everybody wins.  The next evening, the Johnsons led the camp in the inaugural Vespers of the season.  Gary Johnson’s homily focused on the spiritual exploration by Thomas Jefferson, inspired by a recent trip to Monticello in Charlottesville.

The weekly softball game got off to a rousing start with an early staff lead.  Only an inning later, the campers mounted a series of offensive strikes, and never looked back.  Todd Chapple, Kyle Stricker, and Tim Alexander led the way for the campers with Bobby Reigeluth notching the save.  Beware the staff team this year, we’ve got an arsenal of bats ready and loaded.

Speaking of the staff, I’ve already introduced myself as the Coordinator, but I’ve neglected to mention the other members of the Fab Four, otherwise known as The Superfriends.  Megan Collins has returned as one of the Headwaiters, working alongside newcomer Zach Martin.  The final member of the Leadership Team is Andrew Lawson, the Bookkeeper.  Please feel comfortable speaking with any of us at any point so that we may help you enjoy your time in camp this summer.  As for the Wait Staff, we have included photos of each staff member and a short bio on the ICFC website, so go take a gander.  We have a pretty great bunch this summer, I hope you get to meet them all.

I’ll wrap this up with a reminder to call or email the office to make reservations.  It is important to get numbers for the kitchen.  Also, when talking to Florence about getting the cabin ready for arrival, please remember to make a reservation as well.  Okeydokey, time for a swim.  See you on the bay!

Cheers,

Admiral Stewart Bennett, Dinosaur Hunter
